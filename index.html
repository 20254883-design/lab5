Life After Death

Beliefs, Science, and the Unknown

John Carl Cimatu

The idea of life after death has fascinated humanity for thousands of years. Across cultures, people have developed religious and spiritual traditions to explain what happens when we die.

In many faiths, the concept of the soul plays a central role. It is often believed that the soul continues its journey after leaving the physical body, entering realms such as heaven, hell, or reincarnation.

From a psychological perspective, belief in an afterlife can provide comfort and meaning. It helps individuals cope with grief and gives purpose to moral choices in life.

Scientific research into near-death experiences has sparked debates about whether these visions are biological responses of the brain or glimpses of another reality.

Ultimately, the mystery of death remains unsolved. Whether viewed through religion, philosophy, or science, the question challenges us to reflect on our existence and what may lie beyond.
